Questions:

1. Start by reviewing the data.gov home page. What is this?

-Data.gov is a U.S. government website that provides access to public datasets from different federal agencies.

2. Go to Most Viewed Datasets. How many datasets are listed?

-About 50 datasets are listed per page.

3. Find the Topic Category for Human Health. Review the range of data sources
available for this topic. Why do you think these were tagged with the Human Health
category?

-These datasets relate to health, nutrition and food access, so they are categorized under Human Health.

4. Go to the Food Price Outlook. Review the catalog page for this dataset. How can you
navigate to additional datasets from the USDA?

-Click the USDA organization name, use tags, or follow links on the dataset page to browse more USDA data.

6. Try searching for your city, county, and state. What organizations are returned?

-Results include local and state government organizations such as City of Chicago, Cook County, and State of Illinois.

7. Search for USDA, and click into the result. Within the USDA datasets, search for food
composition. Notice that the top three results are all datasets concerning nutrition
information. Try searching the USDA page for nutrition. How does that affect your
results?

-Different keywords return different datasets. “Nutrition" gives broader results than “food composition.”

8a) Try searching for butter, or any of your favorite foods. What kind of results do you
get? What happens when you click on a result?

-You get a list of food items. Clicking one shows detailed nutrition data and components.

8b) Go back to the home page. Find Foundation Foods, and click on Downloads. What
kind of data can you find on this page? How big are these files? Where can you find
more information on the data types available?

-Data is available in CSV and JSON formats. Files are large (MB to GB). More info is in documentation and API pages.
